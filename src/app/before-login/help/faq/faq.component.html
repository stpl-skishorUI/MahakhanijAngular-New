FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Working of the System

A supervisor is a person appointed by the lease owner for a particular plot(s). He creates Transport Permit whenever a vehicle is transporting minerals from source to destination.

Supervsiors are registered into the makhanij system by the lease owner. The lease owner has to login into his account and select "Register Supervisor" form to create supervisor.

A quarry is a specfic plot/site where excavation of mineral takes place.

A lessee or licensee can be a person / organization who receives order for excavation of minerals from the authorised government office.

Permanent lease is the type of lease where the order for mineral excavation and transportion period is given for a longer duration. Permanent Lease orders are given for a period of 3 to 5 years.

Temporary Lease is the type lease where the order for mineral excavation and transportion period is fixed. Temporary lease orders are given for a period of 3 months to 1 year.

A demand note is a sytem generated challan created by department authorities to make a payment. Using this challan the lease holder can make a payment.

RTP stands for Royalty Transport Permit. RTP is issued for quarries where excavation and transportation of minerals is allowed only after paying due royalty charges to the government.

ZRTP stands for Zero Royalty Transport Permit. If the royalty on a mineral is already paid at source location and it needs to be transported in its actual form or in a processed/crushed form, ZRTP is issued for its transportation from the stockpoint or processing plant/crusher. This helps in avoiding duplication of royalty charges on the same mineral. In special cases such as important projects, Government exempts minerals from royalty. In these cases, ZRTP can be issued directly from the Quarries. Government has also announced an exemption of Sand Royalty for Gharkul Scheme beneficiaries. ZRTP is also issued for these beneficiaries.

Applicants needs to fill an online application form (Application >> Interstate Mineral Transport) and pay according to the Demand note for transporting minerals.

The mahakhanij payment is divided into two part: 1. GRAS payment ( Royalty, Grampanchayat tax, Surface rent, ZP tax) 2. mahakhanij payment (DMF, TCS, GST, SI charges and SI GST). The GRAS payment is to be paid via GRAS system and mahakhanij payments need to be paid online or offline through a single transaction.

It is mandatory for the vehicle carrying minerals to enter Maharashtra State through the selected entry point District only.
